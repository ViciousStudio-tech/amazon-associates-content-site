## What to Look for in a Pegboard Organization System

### Material Construction

Steel pegboards last longer and support more weight than composite or hardboard options. Look for powder-coated finishes that resist rust and scratches. In humid climates, this durability difference becomes crucial over time.

### Hook and Accessory Variety  

The best pegboard system is only as good as its accessories. Quality systems include hooks for different tool types, bins for small parts, and specialized holders for items like drill bits or measuring tools. Check what's included versus what you'll need to buy separately.

### Weight Capacity

Consider your heaviest tools when selecting a system. A basic pegboard might handle screwdrivers and pliers fine but fail under the weight of large wrenches or power tools. Professional-grade systems typically support 20-75 lbs per square foot.

### Expandability

Your tool collection will grow over time. Modular systems that accept additional panels or accessories provide better long-term value than fixed-size options. Look for systems where you can add sections without replacing everything.

### Installation Requirements

Some systems mount directly to drywall with included anchors, while others require attachment to wall studs. Consider your garage's construction and your DIY skill level when choosing. Metal systems typically need more robust mounting than lightweight alternatives.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### How much weight can a pegboard hold?

Weight capacity varies dramatically by system type and installation method. Basic hardboard pegboards typically handle 5-10 lbs per hook when properly mounted to studs. Heavy-duty steel systems can support 20-75 lbs per square foot. Always follow manufacturer specifications and use appropriate wall anchors.

### What's the best spacing for pegboard holes?

Standard pegboard uses 1-inch hole spacing, which provides good versatility for most tools. Some specialty systems use different patterns optimized for specific tool types. Unless you have unique requirements, stick with standard spacing for maximum accessory compatibility.

### Can I paint or customize my pegboard?

Most pegboards accept standard paint, but check manufacturer recommendations first. Powder-coated metal systems typically don't need painting but can be customized if desired. Avoid thick paints that might interfere with hook insertion.

### How do I prevent pegboard hooks from falling out?

Quality hooks fit snugly and won't fall out during normal use. If hooks are loose, try different hook styles or consider upgrading to a locking system like StoreWALL's CamLok technology. Some users add small amounts of removable thread locker to problematic hooks.

### What tools work best on pegboards?

Pegboards excel at organizing frequently-used hand tools like screwdrivers, wrenches, pliers, and measuring tools. Power tools work if they're not too heavy and have convenient hanging points. Avoid hanging very heavy items or tools with awkward shapes that don't hang well.
