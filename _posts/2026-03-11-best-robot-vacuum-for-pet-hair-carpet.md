## Buying Guide: What to Look for in a Pet Hair Robot Vacuum

### Suction Power and Brush Design

The most critical factor I've learned is that suction power alone doesn't guarantee success with pet hair on carpet. You need the right combination of suction and brush design. Look for vacuums with at least 1500Pa of suction, but more importantly, focus on dual rubber brushes or self-cleaning brushrolls that won't get tangled with long pet hair.

### Filtration System

Pet dander and allergens are just as important as visible hair. I always recommend vacuums with HEPA filtration systems that can capture particles as small as 0.3 microns. This makes a huge difference in air quality, especially if anyone in your home has allergies.

### Navigation and Mapping

Smart navigation prevents your robot vacuum from getting stuck and ensures complete coverage. While random-pattern vacuums can work, I've found that methodical, room-by-room cleaning patterns are far more effective for consistently removing pet hair from carpets.

### Maintenance Requirements

Consider the ongoing maintenance realistically. Self-emptying bases reduce daily maintenance but come with replacement bag costs. Traditional dustbins require more frequent emptying but have no ongoing costs. Choose based on your lifestyle and preferences.

### Carpet Compatibility

Not all robot vacuums handle carpet equally well. If you have thick, plush carpets, make sure to choose a model specifically rated for medium to high-pile carpets. Low-profile vacuums often struggle with deeper carpet fibers where pet hair likes to hide.
