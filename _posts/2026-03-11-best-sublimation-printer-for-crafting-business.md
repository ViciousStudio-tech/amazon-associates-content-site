## What to Look for in a Sublimation Printer

### Print Size Capabilities

Your target products determine required print sizes. Standard 8.5" x 11" works for most apparel and small items, but custom signs and large transfers need 11" x 17" or 13" x 19" capability.

### Ink System Type

Tank systems like EcoTank models cost more upfront but slash ongoing expenses. Traditional cartridge systems work fine for low-volume operations but become expensive quickly with regular use.

### Conversion Requirements

Purpose-built sublimation printers like Sawgrass models work immediately. Standard inkjet printers need conversion kits and compatible inks - factor this complexity into your decision.

### Print Speed and Quality

Balance speed with quality based on your business model. High-volume, quick-turnaround work benefits from faster printers, while premium custom pieces justify slower, higher-quality output.

### Software and Support

Some printers include design software and color profiles optimized for sublimation. This added value saves time and improves results, especially for beginners.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### What's the difference between regular inkjet and sublimation printers?

Sublimation printers use special inks that turn from solid to gas under heat, bonding with polyester materials. Regular inkjet printers can be converted by replacing standard inks with sublimation inks, but purpose-built sublimation printers offer better results.

### How much does it cost to run a sublimation printer monthly?

Operating costs vary dramatically by printer type and volume. Tank systems like the EcoTank ET-2800 cost around $0.02 per print, while cartridge systems can reach $0.15+ per print. Factor ink, paper, and transfer materials into your pricing calculations.

### Can I use any sublimation ink in these printers?

Converted printers work with most third-party sublimation inks, but Sawgrass printers require proprietary SubliJet inks for warranty protection. Generic inks cost less but may sacrifice color accuracy and print longevity.

### What materials work best with sublimation printing?

Polyester fabrics, polymer-coated ceramics, and specially-treated metals produce the best results. Cotton and natural materials don't hold sublimation inks effectively. Always test new materials before committing to large orders.

### How long do sublimation prints last?

Properly executed sublimation prints on quality materials last years without fading. The ink becomes part of the material rather than sitting on top like traditional printing methods. UV exposure and washing frequency affect longevity.
