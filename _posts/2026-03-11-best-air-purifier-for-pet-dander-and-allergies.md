## Buying Guide: What to Look For

### True HEPA Filtration

When shopping for an air purifier to combat pet dander, True HEPA filtration is non-negotiable. Unlike "HEPA-type" or "HEPA-like" filters, True HEPA filters are certified to capture 99.97% of particles that are 0.3 microns or larger. Since pet dander typically ranges from 0.5 to 100 microns, a True HEPA filter will effectively capture these allergens. I always recommend looking for this certification rather than settling for inferior filtration systems that might miss the microscopic particles causing your allergies.

### Room Size Coverage

Matching your air purifier to your room size is crucial for optimal performance. Manufacturers typically provide coverage areas based on the unit cycling the air 2-3 times per hour. For pet dander removal, I recommend choosing a purifier rated for a slightly larger space than your actual room to ensure more frequent air changes. This approach has proven more effective in my testing, especially in homes with multiple pets or severe allergies.

### Clean Air Delivery Rate (CADR)

The CADR rating tells you how efficiently an air purifier removes specific types of particles. Look for high CADR ratings for dust (which correlates closely with pet dander performance), smoke, and pollen. Higher CADR numbers mean faster air cleaning, which translates to quicker allergy relief. I've found that purifiers with dust CADR ratings above 200 perform noticeably better in homes with pets.

### Noise Levels and Operation Modes

Since air purifiers work best when running continuously, noise levels matter significantly. Look for units with multiple fan speeds and quiet operation, especially if you plan to use the purifier in bedrooms or quiet spaces. Sleep modes and auto-adjust features are valuable for maintaining air quality while minimizing disruption. In my experience, purifiers operating below 50 decibels on their lowest setting are comfortable for nighttime use.

### Filter Replacement Costs and Availability

The ongoing cost of filter replacements can add up quickly, so factor this into your decision. Calculate the annual replacement cost based on the manufacturer's recommendations and filter prices. Some purifiers offer washable pre-filters that can extend the life of the main HEPA filter, reducing long-term costs. I always recommend checking filter availability and pricing before purchasing, as some brands have limited distribution or expensive proprietary filters.
