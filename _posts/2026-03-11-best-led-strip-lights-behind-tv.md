## What to Look for When Buying LED Strip Lights for Your TV

### Length and Coverage

Measure your TV's perimeter before buying. Most 65-inch TVs need about 13-15 feet of strips to go around three sides (left, top, right). Don't try to save money with strips that are too short – gaps in coverage look terrible.

### Color Quality and Brightness

Look for strips with high-quality LEDs that can produce true whites and accurate colors. RGBW strips (with dedicated white LEDs) generally produce better white light than RGB-only strips mixing red, green, and blue.

### Smart Features

Consider what level of automation you want:
- **Basic**: Remote control with preset colors and patterns
- **Smart**: WiFi connectivity with app control and voice commands  
- **Advanced**: Camera sync that responds to TV content in real-time

### Installation Requirements

Check what's included in the box. Quality strips come with:
- Strong 3M adhesive backing
- Corner connectors for clean 90-degree turns
- Adequate power supply for the full strip length
- Clear installation instructions

### Connectivity Options

WiFi strips offer the most features but can be unreliable if your network has issues. Bluetooth strips work well for basic control but lack advanced automation. Thread/Zigbee strips require compatible hubs but offer the most reliable smart home integration.

## Installation Tips from Experience

**Clean the Surface**: Use rubbing alcohol to clean your TV's back panel before applying strips. Dust and oils prevent proper adhesion.

**Plan Your Route**: Start from the bottom corner and work your way around. Most people go counterclockwise, but choose what feels natural for your setup.

**Use Corner Connectors**: Don't bend strips at sharp 90-degree angles. Quality corner connectors prevent LED damage and look cleaner.

**Test Before Final Installation**: Connect everything and test all colors before removing adhesive backing. It's much easier to troubleshoot issues before the strips are permanently mounted.

**Cable Management**: Route power cables behind your entertainment center. Visible cables ruin the clean aesthetic you're trying to achieve.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Do LED strip lights damage TV screens or affect picture quality?

No, properly installed LED strips behind your TV won't damage the screen or affect picture quality. The light is directed away from the screen toward the wall. In fact, bias lighting can improve perceived contrast and reduce eye strain.

### How much power do TV LED strips use?

Most LED strips use 10-30 watts, roughly equivalent to a few standard light bulbs. Smart strips with WiFi connectivity use slightly more power but still represent minimal impact on your electricity bill – usually under $5 per year in additional costs.

### Can I cut LED strips to fit my exact TV size?

Yes, most LED strips have marked cutting points every few LEDs (usually every 2-4 inches). Cut only at these designated spots, and you'll need to properly seal or reconnect the cut end. Some strips include end caps and connectors for this purpose.

### Will LED strips interfere with my TV's remote or WiFi?

Quality LED strips shouldn't interfere with TV remotes or WiFi signals. Cheap strips with poor power filtering might cause issues, which is why I recommend sticking with established brands that include proper electronics and shielding.

### How long do LED strip lights last behind TVs?

Good quality LED strips typically last 25,000-50,000 hours of use. Running them 4 hours daily, you're looking at 15-30 years of lifespan. The adhesive backing usually fails before the LEDs do, which is why quality installation matters.
