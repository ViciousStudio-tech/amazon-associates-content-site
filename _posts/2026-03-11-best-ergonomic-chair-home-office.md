## Buying Guide: What to Look for in an Ergonomic Office Chair Under $500

### Essential Ergonomic Features

When shopping for an ergonomic chair in this price range, focus on these critical features:

**Lumbar Support:** Look for adjustable lumbar support that can be positioned to match your lower back's natural curve. This is non-negotiable for long-term comfort.

**Seat Height Adjustment:** Your chair should allow you to adjust the height so your feet rest flat on the floor with your thighs parallel to the ground.

**Armrest Adjustability:** At minimum, armrests should adjust in height. Ideally, they'll also move in and out or pivot to support various working positions.

**Seat Depth and Width:** The seat should support your thighs without putting pressure behind your knees. A good rule of thumb is 2-3 inches of space between the seat edge and your knees.

### Material Considerations

**Mesh vs. Upholstered:** Mesh backs offer better breathability, while upholstered options often provide more traditional comfort. Consider your office environment and personal preferences.

**Build Quality:** Look for chairs with sturdy bases, smooth-operating mechanisms, and quality casters. These components directly impact longevity.

### Size and Weight Capacity

Always check the manufacturer's weight capacity and dimensions. Most chairs in this price range accommodate users up to 250-300 pounds, but verify this matches your needs.

### Warranty and Return Policies

A good warranty indicates manufacturer confidence in their product. Look for at least a 3-year warranty on major components, with some brands offering longer coverage periods.
