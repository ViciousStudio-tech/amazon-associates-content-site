## What to Look for in an Airbnb Smart Lock

### Remote Access Management

Your chosen smart lock must allow complete remote control from your phone. This means generating new access codes, checking entry logs, and locking/unlocking doors from anywhere. Look for apps that integrate with property management platforms like Airbnb, VRBO, or dedicated rental software.

### Temporary Code Creation

The ability to create time-limited access codes is non-negotiable. These codes should automatically activate at check-in time and deactivate after checkout, preventing unauthorized future access. Some systems allow codes to work only on specific days or during designated hours.

### Battery Life and Monitoring

Smart locks that constantly communicate with your phone or hub drain batteries faster than traditional locks. Look for models advertising 6+ months of battery life, and ensure the system provides low-battery alerts well before complete power loss.

### Weather Resistance

Exterior-mounted keypads face rain, snow, extreme temperatures, and UV exposure. Choose locks rated for your climate conditions. Models with sealed keypads and metal construction typically outlast plastic alternatives in harsh environments.

### Installation Requirements

Consider whether you can install the lock yourself or need professional help. Some models work with existing deadbolts, while others require complete hardware replacement. Factor installation costs and complexity into your decision.

### Guest Accessibility

The lock interface should be intuitive for guests who've never used smart locks. Large, backlit keypads work better than small buttons. Clear instructions and responsive feedback prevent guest frustration and support calls.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### How do I share access codes with Airbnb guests?

Most smart lock apps allow you to send codes directly through messaging, email, or SMS. Many integrate with property management platforms to automatically include codes in check-in instructions. Always send codes 24-48 hours before arrival and confirm guests received them.

### What happens if the smart lock battery dies during a guest stay?

Quality smart locks provide multiple low-battery warnings before complete failure. Most models include backup power options like 9V battery terminals for emergency power. Some guests carry portable phone chargers that can power certain lock models temporarily.

### Can smart locks be hacked or compromised?

Modern smart locks use encrypted communication and require physical proximity for most attacks. Choose locks from established manufacturers that regularly update firmware. Avoid ultra-cheap models that may lack proper security protocols.

### How do I handle guests who can't operate the smart lock?

Keep backup physical keys available for emergencies. Provide clear, simple instructions with photos showing the keypad and code entry process. Consider locks with multiple access methods (keypad, fingerprint, phone app) to accommodate different comfort levels.

### Do smart locks work during power outages?

Battery-powered smart locks continue operating during power outages, though WiFi-dependent features may be unavailable. The physical locking mechanism works normally, but remote access and notifications require internet connectivity.
