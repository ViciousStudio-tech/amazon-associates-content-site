## What to Look for in a Home Office File Cabinet

### Size and Capacity Planning

Before shopping, measure your available floor space and estimate your storage needs. Count your current files and add 50% for future growth. A typical hanging folder holds 20-25 sheets, while expanding files can accommodate 50-100 sheets per section.

### Drawer Configuration: Vertical vs. Lateral

Vertical cabinets save floor space and work well for standard document storage. Lateral cabinets provide easier access and can accommodate unusual document sizes, but require more room. Consider your office layout and how you typically access files.

### Security Features

Lock mechanisms range from simple key locks to digital keypads. Key locks are reliable but require key management. Digital locks offer convenience but need battery maintenance. For highly sensitive documents, consider fireproof options with UL ratings.

### Material and Construction Quality

Steel cabinets offer durability and security at lower cost. Wood cabinets provide aesthetic appeal but require more maintenance. Look for full-extension drawer slides, sturdy handles, and powder-coated finishes for longevity.

### Weight Capacity and Stability

Check both per-drawer and total weight limits. A fully-loaded letter-size drawer can weigh 40-50 pounds. Ensure the cabinet's base is wide enough to prevent tipping, especially with vertical designs.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### How many files can a typical 2-drawer cabinet hold?

A standard 2-drawer vertical cabinet accommodates approximately 120-150 hanging folders or 2,000-3,000 individual documents. This varies based on folder thickness and document density.

### Do I need a locking file cabinet for my home office?

Locking cabinets are essential if you store financial records, client information, or personal documents like social security cards and passports. Even basic key locks deter casual snooping and satisfy many insurance requirements.

### Can file cabinets support weight on top?

Most metal file cabinets can support 50-75 pounds on their surface, making them suitable for printers or office supplies. Always check manufacturer specifications and avoid exceeding weight limits, which can damage drawer mechanisms.

### How do I prevent file cabinet drawers from sticking?

Regular cleaning of drawer tracks and periodic lubrication with white lithium grease prevents most sticking issues. Avoid overloading drawers beyond their rated capacity, and ensure files don't catch on drawer edges.

### What's the difference between letter and legal size compatibility?

Letter-size cabinets (8.5" x 11") are most common and less expensive. Legal-size compatibility (8.5" x 14") requires deeper drawers and costs more but provides flexibility for various document types. Most legal-size cabinets also accommodate letter-size files.
