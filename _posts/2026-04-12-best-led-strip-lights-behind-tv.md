## What to Look for in TV LED Strip Lights

### Length and Coverage

Measure your TV's perimeter before buying. For proper bias lighting, you want strips that extend at least 6 inches beyond each corner. Most 55-65 inch TVs need 13-16 feet of strips for full coverage.

### Color Accuracy and Brightness

Not all LEDs are created equal. Look for strips that specify their color gamut—good ones cover at least 90% of the sRGB color space. Brightness should be adjustable down to very dim levels for comfortable evening viewing.

### Installation Method

Adhesive backing is standard, but quality varies dramatically. Look for 3M adhesive or similar high-quality backing. Some premium options include mounting clips for easier repositioning.

### Smart Features Worth Having

- **App control**: Essential for fine-tuning colors and brightness
- **Voice assistant integration**: Convenient for adjusting during viewing
- **Scheduling**: Automatic dimming for late-night use
- **Music sync**: Fun for parties, though not crucial for TV use

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Do LED strip lights behind TV really reduce eye strain?

Absolutely. Bias lighting reduces the contrast between your bright TV screen and the dark wall behind it. This prevents your pupils from constantly adjusting, which causes fatigue during long viewing sessions. I noticed the difference immediately when I first installed strips behind my bedroom TV.

### How bright should TV bias lighting be?

The ideal brightness is about 10% of your TV's brightness. Most people set their strips too bright initially. Start dim and gradually increase until you find the sweet spot where eye strain is reduced but the lighting isn't distracting.

### Can I cut LED strips to fit my TV exactly?

Most strips can be cut at designated points marked every few inches. However, cutting voids the warranty and requires soldering if you want to reconnect sections. It's usually better to buy the right length and hide excess length behind your TV stand.

### Do smart LED strips slow down my WiFi?

Quality smart strips have minimal impact on WiFi performance. They use very little bandwidth—mostly just connecting for initial setup and periodic updates. Cheap off-brand strips might use outdated protocols that could cause issues.

### How long do LED strip lights last behind TVs?

Good-quality LEDs are rated for 25,000-50,000 hours of use. At 4 hours per day, that's 17-34 years. The adhesive typically fails before the LEDs, but that's usually after 3-5 years depending on temperature and humidity conditions.
