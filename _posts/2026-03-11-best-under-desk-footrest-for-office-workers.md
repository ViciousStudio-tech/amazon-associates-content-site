## Buying Guide: What to Look For

### Height Adjustability

The ability to adjust height is crucial for proper ergonomics. Your footrest should allow your thighs to be parallel to the floor while maintaining a 90-degree angle at your knees. Look for models with multiple height settings or smooth adjustment mechanisms that let you find your perfect position.

### Surface Material and Texture

The surface where your feet rest makes a huge difference in daily comfort. Memory foam offers the best comfort and conformability, while textured surfaces can provide gentle stimulation and prevent feet from sliding. Consider whether you prefer a soft, cushioned feel or a firmer, more supportive surface.

### Size and Footprint

Measure your under-desk space before purchasing to ensure a good fit. Consider both the footrest's dimensions and how much room you'll need to move your feet naturally. Compact designs work well for tight spaces, while larger platforms offer more freedom of movement.

### Stability Features

A footrest that slides around defeats its purpose entirely. Look for non-slip bottoms, adequate weight, or designs that wedge securely under your desk. The last thing you want is to constantly readjust your footrest throughout the day.

### Durability and Build Quality

Since you'll be using your footrest daily, construction quality matters. Check user reviews for long-term durability feedback, and consider materials that can handle regular use without losing their shape or support. Sometimes paying a bit more upfront saves money in the long run.

## Benefits of Using an Under Desk Footrest

Adding a footrest to your workspace setup offers several tangible benefits that go beyond simple comfort. Proper foot positioning helps maintain the natural curve of your spine, reducing strain on your lower back during long sitting sessions. It also promotes better circulation in your legs, which can prevent the swelling and discomfort that comes from prolonged sitting.

Many users report reduced fatigue and increased focus when using a footrest, likely due to improved posture and comfort. The slight elevation also helps prevent the slouching that naturally occurs as we get tired throughout the day.

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

Don't make the mistake of choosing a footrest based solely on price—the cheapest option may not provide adequate support or durability. Also, avoid footrests that are too high or too low for your specific setup, as this can actually worsen your posture rather than improve it.

Another common error is placing the footrest too far under the desk, making it difficult to position your feet naturally. The ideal placement allows your feet to rest comfortably without stretching or straining.
