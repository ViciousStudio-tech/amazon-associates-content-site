## What to Look for When Buying Portable Wedding Lighting

### Power Source Considerations

Battery-powered options offer the most placement flexibility but require charging management. Solar lights work great for all-day events but struggle with overcast weather or shaded venues. I always recommend having backup power banks for critical lighting elements.

### Weather Protection Requirements

Even if your forecast looks perfect, outdoor events demand weatherproof fixtures. Look for IP65 ratings or higher, which protect against rain and dust. I've seen too many beautiful setups ruined by unexpected weather.

### Light Color Temperature

Warm white (2700K-3000K) creates the most romantic, flattering ambiance for weddings. Cool white looks harsh and uninviting in evening settings. Many LED options offer adjustable color temperature – use this feature to match your ceremony and reception moods.

### Installation and Setup Time

Factor setup time into your day-of timeline. Solar lights need afternoon charging time. Battery fixtures require pre-event charging. Complex installations might need professional help or early venue access.

### Quantity Planning

Most couples underestimate how many fixtures they need. For reference, a 100-guest reception typically needs 15-20 string light connections, 8-12 table lighting elements, and 6-8 pathway fixtures for adequate coverage.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### How many lights do I need for a 100-person outdoor wedding?

For adequate coverage, plan on 20-30 lighting elements total. This includes 2-3 string light runs for overhead ambiance, 10-12 table centerpiece lights, 6-8 pathway markers, and 4-6 accent uplights for key areas like the ceremony backdrop.

### Can solar lights work for evening ceremonies?

Yes, but with important caveats. Solar lights need 6-8 hours of direct sunlight for full charge. For 4 PM or later ceremonies, ensure the solar panels get morning and afternoon sun. Always have battery backup options for cloudy day events.

### What's the best way to hang string lights without power outlets?

Use temporary structures like shepherd's hooks, weighted umbrella stands, or tree branches. For venues without hanging points, consider renting light poles or asking your venue about available mounting options. Many couples successfully create stunning overhead displays using creative anchor points.

### How do I prevent lights from blowing out in windy conditions?

Secure all connections with electrical tape and zip ties. Weight down standalone fixtures with sandbags or water weights. For string lights, create slight slack to prevent tension damage. LED bulbs are more wind-resistant than traditional incandescent options.

### Should I hire a professional or DIY my wedding lighting?

DIY works great for basic ambiance using the products I've recommended. Consider professionals for complex installations, venues with electrical complications, or if you want synchronized light shows. Most couples successfully handle portable battery and solar options themselves.
