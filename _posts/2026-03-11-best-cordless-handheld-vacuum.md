## Buying Guide: What to Look For

### Battery Life and Power

When choosing a cordless handheld vacuum, I always prioritize battery performance. Look for lithium-ion batteries that provide at least 10-15 minutes of continuous runtime. Higher voltage typically means more power, but also consider how the vacuum maintains suction as the battery depletes.

### Weight and Ergonomics

Since you'll be holding the vacuum during use, weight matters significantly. I recommend staying under 3 pounds for comfortable extended use. The handle design and weight distribution also affect user fatigue during longer cleaning sessions.

### Dustbin Capacity and Emptying

A larger dustbin means fewer interruptions during cleaning, but it also adds weight. I prefer models with one-touch emptying mechanisms that minimize contact with debris. Clear or translucent bins help you monitor capacity levels.

### Filtration System

Quality filtration is crucial, especially if you have allergies. HEPA filters capture the smallest particles, while washable filters reduce long-term costs. Consider how easy it is to access and clean the filters.

### Attachments and Accessories

Different cleaning tasks require specialized tools. Crevice tools help reach tight spaces, while brush attachments work well on upholstery. Pet owners should prioritize motorized brush tools for effective hair removal.

### Charging Time and Convenience

Fast charging capabilities minimize downtime between uses. Wall-mounted charging stations keep the vacuum accessible and ensure it's always ready when needed.
